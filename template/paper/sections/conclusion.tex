\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The MLP is a small model with a clean story.
It can approximate any continuous function (\cref{thm:ua}), it trains by
backpropagation (\cref{alg:train}), and on this task its accuracy saturates once
the hidden layers reach a few hundred units (\cref{fig:width}).
Width buys generalization cheaply at first and then stops paying.
The obvious next step is to check whether that plateau moves with harder data or
with normalization between layers.
