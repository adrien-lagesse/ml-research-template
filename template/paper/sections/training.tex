\section{Training}
\label{sec:training}

We fit $\vtheta$ by minimizing the empirical risk
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:risk}
  \hat{\vtheta} = \argmin_{\vtheta}\; \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n}
    \ell\!\left(\mathrm{MLP}_{\vtheta}(\vx_i),\, y_i\right),
\end{equation}
where $\ell$ is the softmax cross-entropy over the $n$ training pairs.
\Cref{eq:risk} has no closed form, so we descend it with minibatch stochastic
gradient descent, taking each gradient by backpropagation~\citep{rumelhart1986}.
\Cref{alg:train} is one full run.

Two choices matter more than the optimizer itself.
We initialize weights with the scheme of \citet{he2015}, which keeps activations
from collapsing through a deep rectifier stack; the older proposal of
\citet{glorot2010} works nearly as well at this depth.
Adam~\citep{kingma2015} and plain SGD reached the same accuracy on our task, so we
report SGD.
Every run uses a learning rate of \num{e-3}, a batch size of \num{128}, and
\num{50} epochs.
