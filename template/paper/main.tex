\begin{abstract}
  The multilayer perceptron is the simplest deep network, and it still repays
  study. We review its definition and the universal approximation property,
  describe minibatch training by backpropagation, and measure how hidden width
  shapes generalization on a synthetic classification task. Accuracy rises
  quickly with width and then plateaus: past a few hundred hidden units, extra
  capacity buys almost nothing. We report the training dynamics, the width
  sweep, and a small benchmark of depth--width configurations.
\end{abstract}
